
## Task 7 – Team Collaboration Reflection

### Reflective Report – Group: Versatile

**Week 2 Machine Learning Assignment – Team Reflection**

This week's assignment challenged our team to move beyond theoretical understanding and apply machine learning concepts practically — from coding a regression model using only NumPy to designing a multi-model comparison strategy. The process was educational not just in terms of technical knowledge, but also in terms of collaborative workflow and professional communication.

**Task Division and Contributions**

We divided the seven tasks based on individual strengths and learning goals. Our team lead took ownership of the linear regression implementation (Task 2) and Explainable AI (Task 5), given their deeper background in mathematical modeling and AI ethics. A second member focused on writing the model evaluation theory (Task 1) and building the classification model (Task 3). A third member handled the model selection comparison table (Task 4) and academic documentation. The fourth member managed the GitHub repository setup (Task 6) and compiled this reflection (Task 7).

**Challenges Encountered**

Our first major challenge was the linear regression implementation — avoiding the temptation to import scikit-learn and instead computing every value manually using NumPy. Deriving the slope and intercept from the Ordinary Least Squares formula required returning to mathematical fundamentals, which proved to be one of the most rewarding parts of the assignment.

In Task 3, we encountered a class imbalance issue — the synthetic dataset produced significantly fewer defaulters than non-defaulters. We addressed this through stratified splitting and acknowledged it as a real-world challenge in the output explanation.

**Lessons Learned**

The most important lesson was the value of metric selection. Our analysis of the hospital scenario (99% accuracy with 38% recall) profoundly reshaped how each team member evaluates model performance. We learned that a single metric never tells the full story.

We also gained practical experience with GitHub collaboration — creating structured repositories, writing meaningful READMEs, and maintaining clean commit histories. The Explainable AI task opened our eyes to the ethical dimensions of machine learning, reminding us that deploying AI responsibly requires transparency alongside accuracy.

As a team, we grow stronger each week — not only in technical skill, but in our understanding that great AI engineering requires both rigor and responsibility.
